Project Ask

Using a mobile application, how can we solve a problem faced by a local Pittsburgh company?

Project Overview

For my Interaction Design studio, teams were randomly assigned to a local Pittsburgh company. My team was assigned Construction Junction, which is a large non-profit warehouse supplying surplus construction materials & appliances at reduced prices.

Impact

We presented our work to the Construction Junction management and were able to create some movement into how they partner with the local Pittsburgh community!

02 Solution

To address the knowledge gap in the community about Construction Junction's larger mission of promoting conservation and reuse, my team and I came up with Junction Journey: a mobile application geared towards kids that uses gamification and learning science principles to create a fun environment for children to learn about sustainability and conservation.

Feel free to click around on our Framer prototype!






Junction Journey would not only be training the next generation, but would
also be penetrating a much larger audience in Pittsburgh: schools and
educators - simply by their interaction with children on a daily basis.

03 Approach

understanding the business model

We took a look at Construction Junctionâ€™s goals and business model, their mission and products, and how they are involved with the community, since they are a non-profit organization. Upon initial analysis, we found that many community members refer to Construction as the "junkyard".


problem discovery

After doing some analysis we realized that the Construction Junction business model has a two-fold purpose: buying and selling reused goods along with promoting reuse and recycling in the Pittsburgh community through facility tours and workshops.

You can see this even by taking a quick look at their website and social media - more than half of the information is dedicated to this mission of reuse.



The key takeaway was that they market themselves as mostly a buying and selling platform. They are currently not promoting events and their conservation goals on any other site except for their main website, which is visited least often in comparison to Facebook and Instagram.


Stakeholder Analysis

We then proceeded to understand the stakeholders of Construction Junction, paring down the community into four main groups internal and external to Construction Junction:

Environment - the companies that have partnerships with CJ and have similar missions
Internal - the multiple groups that work together to help Construction Junction function properly
Education - home to 68 colleges and universities, targeting students, teachers, and faculty
Local - groups that Construction Junction caters to such as the government, homeowners, and DIYers. On the map, larger bubbles indicate opportunities and overlaps represent parties that have some connection to each other.




Narrowing the Problem

From the social media analysis, it was evident that Construction Junction is not successful in promoting their events and goals to help teach the community about reuse. Thus, our team narrowed it down to two main spaces: focusing on environmental groups and education.



We chose to focus on the education market for several reasons over environments:
Reach to a much larger group of people (parents, students at varying levels, teachers, faculty, coaches
Give back to the community of Pittsburgh with 68+ schools and universities
Excitement for potential opportunities that could lie ahead


03 Ideation

After narrowing down on the Education space, our team had to come up with some way to use a mobile service to engage educators and students with conservation education. We brainstormed questions in four categories to identify key opportunities.



We also used Reverse Assumptions to understand what kinds of mobile services would help us answer these questions




The reverse assumptions exercise was helpful in coming up with ideas for our service. Since community is really important to CJ and host a variety of educational opportunities such as site visits, we decided to hone in on schools and particularly the idea of conservation education. We came up with different problem areas within education.

Problem Statement Development

Our team came up with 20+ problem statements, voted on our top three, and combined them into one central problem statement.

Top Three:
We don't have resuability drives in local schools
Schools don't understand exactly what we can do for them/how they could benefit
We don't have a virtual presence with the younger population of Pittsburgh

Combined Statement:
We don't have a virtual presence with local students in Pittsburgh to share and engage them in our vision

Roundtable Brainstorming

We first did a roundtable brainstorm session. Each person in our group was given one page and 60 seconds to draw potential ideas for the screens. Then we rotated when the timer rang. This helped us get all of our thoughts out on paper, so we could have a meaningful discussion about the direction of our mobile app. We also created plans for how we wanted to setup our app prior to low-fi sketching.
