Internship Overview

This summer, I had the incredible opportunity to be a UX Designer for the SPIFe (Scheduling and Planning System for Exploration) team at the NASA AMES Research Center. The team had a work process similar to that of a startup embedded within a larger organization. I worked on two main projects: NASA HUNCH and Playbook. Over the summer, I grew my technical design knowledge and was able to learn how to work with software developers in an agile development environment. I'm going to take a deep dive into Playbook and summarize my work with NASA HUNCH.

Project Ask

Create Hotkeys and Shortcuts for the most implemented actions in the Playbook, evaluate and redesign the system structure of the Playbook iPad application.

Project Overview

Playbook is an intuitive software designed to help astronauts edit certain adjustable activitiesin space or on other analog missions, without needing to rely on mission control everytime there is a small change. This has a useful application in missions where there is a time lag between mission control (Earth) and the astronauts who may be on the Moon or Mars. Currently, this software is used on analog missions(field tests in locations that have physical similarities to extreme space environments) such as NASA NEEMO, but the hope is our software can be used for Mars 2020 and the like!

03 System Design

As I was playing around with the tester site, I noticed that it took a considerable number of clicks to commit to a certain action. I noticed this had a lot to do with the design of the Playbook system, specifically having an action tray - a tray that pops out when you need to make edits to the schedule. Before going into my main tasking, I decided to see if we could clean up the user flow of editing something.





The proposed user flow cuts the number of clicks by 44%! Although it's only a few clicks change per action, it easily adds up as crewmembers are constantly changing their schedule throughout spaceflight. The key changes were repurposing the activity tray as a space where actions can also occur to activities and not just as an informational space. This eliminated the need for pop-ups and the like. Additionally, cutting the preferences to the bare minimum really helped in saving clicks (for example having an entry rather than click through a long list of numbers).

05 NASA HUNCH

Overview

NASA HUNCH is a website that aims to empower and inspire high school students and give them the opportunity to launch their careers through participation in the design and fabrication of real world valued products for NASA. The goal of the site is to be a platform to raise awareness of the International Space Station, current HUNCH projects, and to enable students to showcase their work and connect with NASA social media sites.I was tasked with revamping and creating new webpages for the site. Specifically, I built a new social media pagethat allowed easy access to NASA HUNCH's social media channels and also built a blog page where schoolscould showcase the work of their students and be inspired by the work of other educator s across the country. I was able to learn a lot about APIs and Wordpress (Elementor) plug-insthrough this project, as we were trying to create a template that could be edited by high schools without coding.



Initial Browsing

I initially spent some time scouring through the current HUNCH site, trying to understand what HUNCH was all about and how they intended to get their message across through their website. From reading each page, looking at the layout and tabs, and identifying the type of information available on the site, I found there to be a couple key goals for the HUNCH site:

Key Goals
01 Empower high school students to get involved regardless of technical level
02 Build a larger network of involvement with NASA projects in the public
03 Find top talent and be able to connect with them for future work
04 Push social media and awareness of NASA HUNCH

Additionally, there were several problems that came up in my research.

General Issues
01 No about page explaining the background/goals of HUNCH
02 Schools do not currently have a way to share their process with other schools

Standardization Issues
01 Differing sizing on each page and different layouts
02 Some pages are extremely fleshed out and others barely have any content
03 Issues with spacing and a “pixel perfect” look
04 Portraying a common message through the site

I sought to work on some of these issues through the two pages I was assigned.

06 Future Steps + Reflection

Currently, the work is being implemented, so the future steps would be to ensure that everything is deployed successfully. The site is currently being worked on and the changes on Playbook are going to be implemented for the next NEEMO mission. This was my first UX internship and the first time I actually implemented my designs beyond the classroom. Therefore, there was a huge learning curve for me in terms of being able to work directly with software engineers on implementation in an Agile environment. What I realized is that things often have to be spelled out or extremely pixel perfect and specified for a software engineer to carry out a design exactly as it is meant to be. Outside of improving my design skills, working with JIRA and understanding how to document interactions and specifications were some of the most valuable things I learned during this internship.
